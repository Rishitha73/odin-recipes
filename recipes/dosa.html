Dosa: The Crispy, Savory South Indian Delight

Dosa is a quintessential South Indian dish known for its thin, crispy texture and savory flavor. Made from a fermented batter of rice and urad dal (black gram), dosas are often served with a variety of accompaniments such as sambar (a spicy lentil stew), coconut chutney, and tomato chutney. Whether enjoyed plain, stuffed with spiced potatoes (masala dosa), or served with a range of fillings, dosa is a versatile and beloved staple in Indian cuisine, celebrated for its delicious taste and satisfying crunch.

Recipe:

Ingredients

  • 1 cup rice(preferably short-grain or parboiled rice)
  • 1/4 cup urad dal(split black gram)
  • 1/4 cup fenugreek seeds(optional, helps with fermentation)
  • water(for soaking and blending)
  • salt(to taste)

Steps

Preparing for Dosa batter

  1. Soak the Ingredients:
    • Rinse 1 cup of rice and 1/4 cup of urad dal (and fenugreek seeds, if using) in plenty of water.
    • Soak them separately in water for at least 4-6 hours or overnight.
  2. Blend the Batter:
    • Drain the soaked rice and urad dal.
    • In a blender or wet grinder, combine the rice and urad dal with enough water to make a smooth batter. The consistency should be slightly thicker than pancake batter.
    • Transfer the batter to a large bowl, cover, and let it ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours, or until it has doubled in volume and has a slightly sour aroma.
  3. Add Salt:
    • Once fermented, gently stir the batter and add salt to taste.

Making Dosa

  1. Heat the Pan:
    • Heat a non-stick or cast-iron skillet over medium heat.
    • Lightly grease the pan with a paper towel dipped in oil or ghee.
  2. Pour the Batter:
    • Pour a ladleful of batter onto the center of the pan.
    • Using the back of the ladle, spread the batter in a thin, even circle, starting from the center and moving outward in a spiral motion.
  3. Cook the Dosa:
    • Drizzle a little oil or ghee around the edges of the dosa.
    • Cook until the dosa turns golden brown and crispy, and the edges start to lift from the pan (about 2-3 minutes).
    • Flip the dosa if you prefer it crispier, or serve it as is if you like it softer.
  4. Serve:
    • Remove the dosa from the pan and serve hot with sambar, coconut chutney, and tomato chutney

Enjoy your delicious homemade dosas!
