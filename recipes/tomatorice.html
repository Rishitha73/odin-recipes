Tomato Rice: Flavorful and Fragrant Comfort Dish

Tomato Rice, a beloved staple in South Indian cuisine, is a delicious and aromatic one-pot rice dish infused with the rich flavors of ripe tomatoes and a blend of spices. This vibrant dish features tender rice cooked with a tangy tomato base, seasoned with ingredients like onions, garlic, ginger, and spices such as cumin, coriander, and turmeric. Often garnished with fresh herbs and sometimes topped with fried onions or nuts, Tomato Rice is both comforting and versatile, perfect for a quick weeknight meal or a delightful addition to any meal spread. Its comforting, slightly tangy flavor profile makes it a favorite among rice dishes.

Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 cup basmati rice (or any long-grain rice)
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 1 large onion (finely chopped)
  • 2-3 green chilies (slit or chopped, adjust to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 large tomatoes (finely chopped)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar (optional, for balancing acidity)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 cup water (adjust as needed)
  • A few curry leaves (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice (optional, for extra tanginess)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Rice:
    • Rinse 1 cup of basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear.
    • Soak the rice in water for 15-20 minutes, then drain and set aside
  2. Cook the Base:
    • Heat 2 tablespoons of oil or ghee in a large pan or pressure cooker over medium heat.
    • Add 1 teaspoon of mustard seeds and let them splutter.
    • Add 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds and sauté for a few seconds.
  3. Sauté the Aromatics:
    • Add the finely chopped onion and green chilies to the pan. Sauté until the onions become translucent.
    • Stir in 1 tablespoon of ginger-garlic paste and cook until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add Tomatoes and Spices:
    • Add the finely chopped tomatoes to the pan. Cook until the tomatoes soften and start to break down.
    • Stir in 1/2 teaspoon of turmeric powder, 1 teaspoon of red chili powder, 1 teaspoon of coriander powder, and salt to taste. Cook the mixture until the oil starts to separate from the masala.
  5. Combine Rice and Water:
    • Add the drained rice to the pan and mix gently to coat the rice with the tomato masala.
    • Pour in 1/2 cup of water (adjust the amount depending on the rice variety and cooking method) and add a pinch of sugar if desired. Stir well.
  6. Cook the Rice:
    • If using a pan, cover with a tight-fitting lid and cook on low heat for 15-20 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and all the water is absorbed.
    • If using a pressure cooker, close the lid and cook on low heat for 2 whistles (or according to the manufacturer’s instructions). Turn off the heat and let the pressure release naturally.
  7. Finish and Garnish:
    • Once the rice is cooked, gently fluff it with a fork.
    • Stir in a few curry leaves if using, and garnish with chopped fresh coriander leaves.
    • Add 1 tablespoon of lemon juice if you prefer extra tanginess.
  8. Serve:
    • Serve Tomato Rice hot, garnished with fried onions or nuts if desired. It pairs well with yogurt, raita, or a simple salad.

Enjoy your flavorful and aromatic Tomato Rice!
