Pulihora: Tangy and Spiced South Indian Rice

Pulihora, also known as Puliyodarai or Tamarind Rice, is a vibrant and flavorful South Indian dish celebrated for its tangy, spicy profile. This rice dish is prepared by mixing cooked rice with a tangy tamarind sauce, tempered with a blend of aromatic spices, including mustard seeds, curry leaves, and peanuts. The addition of dried red chilies and a touch of jaggery enhances the depth of flavor, balancing the tartness with a hint of sweetness. Often garnished with fresh coriander leaves and served as a comforting meal or festive treat, Pulihora is a beloved staple in South Indian cuisine, cherished for its bold flavors and satisfying taste.

Recipe

Ingredients

For the Tamarind Mixture:

  • 1/4 cup tamarind pulp (extracted from 1-2 tablespoons of tamarind paste or concentrate)
  • 1 tablespoon oil (for tempering)
  • 1 tablespoon chana dal (split chickpeas)
  • 1 tablespoon urad dal (split black gram)
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1-2 dried red chilies (broken into pieces)
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon jaggery (or brown sugar, optional, for a touch of sweetness)
  • A few curry leaves
  • Salt(to taste)

For the Rice:

  • 2 cups cooked rice (preferably slightly cooled; can use white or brown rice)
  • 2 tablespoons oil (for mixing with the rice)
  • 1 tablespoon roasted peanuts (optional, for added crunch)
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

Optional Add-Ins:

  • 1/4 cup grated coconut (for added flavor)
  • 1 teaspoon sesame seeds (for extra texture)

Note:

Feel free to adjust the amount of tamarind pulp and jaggery according to your taste preferences.

Steps

  1. Prepare the Tamarind Mixture:
    • In a small bowl, dissolve the tamarind pulp in a bit of water if it’s too thick. Set aside.
  2. Temper the Spices:
    • Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a pan over medium heat.
    • Add the mustard seeds and let them splutter.
    • Add the chana dal and urad dal, and sauté until they turn golden brown.
    • Add the broken dried red chilies and curry leaves. Sauté for a few seconds until fragrant.
    • Add the turmeric powder and mix well.
  3. Add Tamarind Mixture:
    • Pour the tamarind pulp into the pan.
    • Stir in the jaggery (if using) and salt to taste.
    • Cook the mixture on medium heat, stirring occasionally, until it thickens slightly and the raw smell of tamarind disappears (about 8-10 minutes).
  4. Combine with Rice:
    • In a large mixing bowl, add the cooked rice and 2 tablespoons of oil.
    • Pour the tamarind mixture over the rice.
    • Gently mix the rice and tamarind mixture until the rice is evenly coated.
  5. Add Optional Ingredients:
    • If using, stir in the roasted peanuts, grated coconut, and sesame seeds.
  6. Garnish and Serve:
    • Garnish with chopped fresh coriander leaves.
    • Serve warm or at room temperature with additional side dishes if desired.

Enjoy your delicious Pulihora, a tangy and spiced South Indian rice dish!
